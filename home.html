Misson: To Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources for sustainable development

Fishing: If we fish irresponsibly, we can kill many marine species, threatening our ecosystem and potentially leading to seafood shortages.

Link to an article on destructive fishing: https://marine-conservation.org/end-destructive-fishing/

Pollution: Debris in the water can cause marine life to either die or consume large quantities of microplastics, making them harmful to consume.

Excess nutrients: Usually caused by humans, causes large algae blooms to overtake sections of the water and induce hypoxia, rapidly depleting the oxygen supply for sea animals.

Link to an article on pollution and excess nutrients:https://www.noaa.gov/education/resource-collections/ocean-coasts/ocean-pollution#:~:text=Heavy%20metals%20and%20other%20contaminants,food%20out%20of%20the%20water

The studies of marine mammals are joined with the studies of humans, such as the consumption of microplastics, or how a whales long breath underwater relates to its ‘cancer-resisting cells’. We can learn a lot from animals, and can even save humans by using this research.

Medicine comes from the ocean. Pain killers, anti-bacterial and anti-tumor agents are all elements that are found from sea creatures and extracted for the development of medicines for us humans.

Link to an article on medicine from the ocean: https://oceanexplorer.noaa.gov/facts/medicinesfromsea.html
